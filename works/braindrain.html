Brain Drain

"Brain Drain" is an interactive audiovisual installation, conceived and presented by students of Digital Design and Media, Acoustics and Music Technology, and Sound Design during my MSc in Edinburgh.

For this work, a number of physical sound objects were created which would be triggered/operated/played by members of the installation audience.

These objects consisted of assemblages of mostly found, scrapped, or self-built devices, such as old record players, DIY loudspeakers, and suchlike - and triggering mechanisms ranging from potentiometer-operated wires to laser microphones, Arduinos and a variety of sensors.

Sound was further distributed by way of an eight-speaker setup connected to a Max/MSP patch that allows free spatial placement of sound sources on a horizontal plane, plus an additional pair of GEL surface speakers allowing sound to be played through two suspended metal sheets.

The effects of the ensuing aural stimuli on the affective state of a blindfolded person located in the centre of the installation space were tracked by means of a portable EEG-device, and projected as abstract visualisations.

Installation conceived, designed and built by: Robbie Christie, Qianqian Hu, Asuka Kanetake, Zechao Li, Marina Papagianni, Wolfgang Thomas, Donghui Xie
